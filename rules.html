Non-Negotiable Rules

These rules protect you. Follow them without exception.

1 Never mention this programme to anyone at your company
Do not mention the programme name, your coach's name, "beta cohort," template numbers, or the fact that you are being coached by anyone external. This applies to everyone — your manager, HR, colleagues, friends at work. No exceptions.
DON'T: "I'm in a coaching programme and my coach suggested I create this proposal."
DO: "I've been working on my professional development and put together a proposal for UI automation."
Why: Your employer may have policies about external parties influencing internal project decisions. Even if they don't, the perception alone can trigger HR concerns. Protect yourself.
2 Every document you share at work must be a clean copy
Before sharing any document internally, remove ALL traces of this programme: template numbers (T1-T12), programme branding, coach's name, worked example links, "Week X Asset" labels, and the footer. Create a separate clean copy — never share the original template.
DON'T: Share a document titled "QEA Template 4 — The 1-Page Initiative Pitch Template" with the footer "Coached by [name]"
DO: Share a document titled "UI Automation Initiative Proposal" with no programme references anywhere
Why: A single reference can raise questions you don't want to answer. It takes 2 minutes to clean a document. It takes weeks to recover from an HR escalation.
3 Never paste programme content into company AI tools
If your company provides ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude Team, Copilot for Business, or any enterprise AI tool — do NOT paste any programme templates, the AI coaching card, or any document with programme references into it. Company admins can see your prompts and conversations.
DON'T: Paste "You are my technical implementation coach for the [programme name]..." into your company's ChatGPT Enterprise
DO: Use your personal AI accounts (free or paid) for programme-related work. Or use the cleaned version of the AI coaching card which has no programme references.
Why: Enterprise AI tools give company admins visibility into what you type. If your prompt contains a coach's name or programme name, it becomes discoverable.
4 Present templates as your own professional thinking
The templates are frameworks to help you think. The output is YOUR work. When you share a stakeholder map, an ADR, or a status update at work, you're not "using a template from a programme" — you're "putting together a structured approach because you're a professional."
DON'T: "Here's Template 6, the Stakeholder Influence Map I filled in."
DO: "I mapped out the key people we need on board for this initiative."
Why: Nobody questions where a senior engineer learned to think strategically. They just see someone operating at a higher level. The templates are invisible scaffolding — the output looks like your own thinking, because it is.
5 If anyone asks, you're doing self-directed professional development
If a colleague, manager, or HR ever asks where you learned a framework or approach, your answer is: "I've been investing in my professional development — reading, learning, applying." That's completely true. You don't owe anyone details about how you learn.
DON'T: "I'm in an external programme with a coach who's guiding me through 12 templates."
DO: "I've been doing some professional development on my own — learning how to structure and present initiatives better."
Why: "Professional self-development" is something every company encourages. It raises zero flags. The moment you mention an external programme, it creates questions about IP, conflicts of interest, and who's influencing your work.
Pre-Share Checklist — Use Before Sharing ANY Document at Work
  • Created a separate clean copy (NOT the original template)
  • Removed all programme branding, template numbers (T1-T12), and "Week X Asset" labels
  • Removed coach's name and any mention of external coaching
  • Removed worked example links
  • Removed the footer line
  • Gave the document a natural title (e.g., "UI Automation Proposal" not "Template 4")
  • Can explain every single line without referencing the programme
  • Have NOT pasted any programme content into company AI tools
! Participant Safety Note
These frameworks are for your professional development. You own the decision to share, adapt, or withhold them at work. Use only what you are permitted to use within your employer's policies. This programme helps you think, communicate, and lead more effectively within whatever rules your employer sets — it does not ask you to bypass any company process, policy, or governance structure. If in doubt about what's appropriate to share at your company, err on the side of keeping it private and showing results instead.
